# Korean Reader App - Learning Guide 📚

## 🎯 How This App Helps You Learn Korean

The Korean Reader App is specifically designed to accelerate your Korean language learning journey through intelligent morphological analysis and comprehensive dictionary support.

### Learning Benefits

#### 1. **Understand Korean Grammar Structure**
- Korean is an agglutinative language where words are built by adding particles and endings to base forms
- Our app automatically breaks down complex sentences into individual morphemes
- See exactly how particles (조사) attach to nouns and how endings conjugate verbs/adjectives

#### 2. **Learn Word Base Forms**
- Automatically converts conjugated forms to dictionary base forms (stemming)
- Example: "공부합니다" → "공부하다" (to study)
- This helps you look up words in dictionaries more effectively

#### 3. **Multi-Language Support**
- Definitions available in:
  - 🇲🇳 Mongolian - For Mongolian speakers learning Korean
  - 🇬🇧 English - For international learners
  - 🇰🇷 Korean - For immersion and advanced learners
- Part of speech tags help you understand word usage
- Example sentences show words in context

#### 4. **Offline Learning**
- Study anywhere without internet connectivity
- Perfect for commuting, traveling, or areas with limited connectivity
- All 30,000+ dictionary entries available instantly

## 📖 Step-by-Step Learning Method

### Beginner Level (Level 1)

**Step 1: Start Simple**
```
저는 학생입니다
(나는 학생이다 - I am a student)
```
- Tap each word to see its meaning
- Notice how "-는" attaches to "저" (I/me)
- Notice how "-입니다" is the polite form of "이다" (to be)

**Step 2: Understand Particles**
```
한국어를 공부해요
(한국어 + 를 = Korean + object particle)
```
- "를" marks "한국어" as the object of the verb
- Tap "를" to learn it's an object particle (목적격 조사)

**Step 3: Learn Verb Conjugations**
```
먹습니다, 먹어요, 먹었다
```
- All these forms stem from "먹다" (to eat)
- The app shows the base form when you tap any conjugation
- Learn different politeness levels and tenses

### Intermediate Level (Level 2-3)

**Step 4: Complex Sentences**
```
학교에 가서 친구를 만났습니다
```
- Break down: 학교 + 에, 가 + 아서, 친구 + 를, 만나 + 았습니다
- Learn connective endings like "-아서/어서" (and then)
- Understand past tense "-았/었-"

**Step 5: Advanced Vocabulary**
- Access Level 3-4 vocabulary with detailed definitions
- Learn grammar patterns specific to advanced communication
- Study example sentences for proper usage

### Advanced Level (Level 4)

**Step 6: Nuanced Expressions**
- Explore subtle differences between similar expressions
- Learn formal vs. informal speech levels
- Understand cultural context through examples

## 💡 Learning Tips

### Daily Practice Routine

1. **Morning Session (10 minutes)**
   - Analyze 3-5 simple sentences
   - Review new vocabulary from previous day
   - Focus on particle usage

2. **Afternoon Session (15 minutes)**
   - Work with longer, complex sentences
   - Study verb conjugations in different tenses
   - Use the app while reading Korean news or webtoons

3. **Evening Review (10 minutes)**
   - Re-analyze morning sentences without looking at results first
   - Test yourself: can you identify all morphemes?
   - Note difficult words for tomorrow's review

### Effective Study Techniques

**Active Recall:**
- Before tapping a word, try to guess its meaning
- Cover the definition and test yourself
- Say the word aloud with correct pronunciation

**Spaced Repetition:**
- Review words you looked up today
- Revisit them tomorrow, then in 3 days, then in a week
- The app's search history (planned feature) will help with this

**Context Learning:**
- Don't just memorize isolated words
- Study words within complete sentences
- Notice how particles change meaning

**Particle Mastery:**
- Focus on one particle type per week
- Week 1: Topic particles (은/는)
- Week 2: Object particles (을/를)
- Week 3: Location particles (에/에서)
- Week 4: Possessive particles (의)

## 🎓 Educational Features Explained

### Morphological Analysis
Korean words don't exist in isolation. They combine with grammatical markers:
- **Nouns** + Particles (조사) = Grammatical function
- **Verbs/Adjectives** + Endings (어미) = Tense, politeness, mood

Our app reveals these combinations so you can:
- Identify the root word
- Understand what each suffix means
- Build your own sentences correctly

### Dictionary Levels
The app includes vocabulary organized by proficiency:
- **Level 1**: Basic everyday words (~5,000 entries)
- **Level 2**: Intermediate vocabulary (~8,000 entries)
- **Level 3**: Advanced business/academic words (~10,000 entries)
- **Level 4**: Specialized and literary terms (~7,000 entries)

Start with Level 1 and progressively challenge yourself!

### Example Sentences
Each dictionary entry includes example sentences showing:
- Proper word usage in context
- Common collocations (words that go together)
- Different sentence patterns
- Cultural nuances
